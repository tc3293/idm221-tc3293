About me

My name is Tony Chen. I am a transfer student from a community college in Salisbury, Maryland. Today, I'm currently a sophomore at Drexel University. My family is originally from China and moved to America in 2006. My father works presently at East Moon, a Japanese restaurant. When I was young, my parents separated, and my mother chose to stay in China. I spend most of my life with my father.

I have worked to support my family since I was 14 years old. I work on weekends as a packer at my father's workplace. We moved around a lot ever since we arrived in America to give us better opportunities. As the oldest child, I will be the first in our family to attend university.

I was born with severe damage to my hearing. At age two, I got a high fever that caused this severe damage to my hearing. I wear hearing aids all the time. Sometimes I cannot hear clearly at distances, even if people shout at me. My hearing loss negatively impacted my life because asking people to repeat what they say can be irritating.

It is hard to communicate or have conversations with people when I do not hear clearly and miss several words. It isn't easy to hear clear when people speak fast. It is slightly more complex and confusing when a person is whispering to me in a crowded place. I also misunderstand conversations when words have similar pronunciations, like "Prey and pray."
